8. Mention parts of a seed and their functions. 2. Explain stages of germination. 3. How animals help seed dispersal 4. What are characteristics of flowers that pollinated by insect? 5. Why are flowers very important for plants?​


Jawaban:

Germination is the emergence of new individuals from seeds. The types of germination are hypogel and epigeal. Hypogeal is a type of germination in which the cotyledons are still in the soil surface. Epigeal is a type of germination where the cotyledons are above the soil surface. Hypogeal occurs in monocot plants, while epigeal occurs in dicots.

Discussion

Germination is the process of embryo growth in seeds to form new plants. Seeds start to germinate after absorbing water from the surrounding environment which is called imbibition. The water that enters the seeds will stimulate hormones and enzymes to work so that the embryos in the seeds begin to grow. Dormancy is an event that the seeds have not yet undergone germination (rest period).

1. The germination process begins with the entry of water into the seeds. Dry seeds will absorb water from the soil or the surrounding environment. The process of water absorption by the seeds is called imbibition.

2. After the seeds absorb water, the size of the seeds will expand and enlarge causing the seed coat to break sometimes.

3. The incoming water will trigger the activation of the gibberellin hormone in the embryo, this hormone will then trigger the cells in the aleurone layer to produce the enzyme amylase.

4. The amylase enzyme that has been produced will work in the endosperm (food reserves) to convert starch into sugar.

5. Then the sugar that has been produced will be transferred to the embryo as material for embryo growth.

Aniosperm plant seeds or closed seeds can be divided into two, namely dicots and monocots. This difference is based on the number of seed chips the seed has. Dicot is a seed that has two or more seeds, while monocot is a seed that has only one seed.

The parts of dicot and monocot seeds are almost the same but also have some differences, including:

A. Part of dicot seeds (epigeal type)

1. Seed sheath is a thin membrane that protects the seeds from physical damage and prevents excessive water evaporation.

2. The cotyledons are the largest part of the seed and contain food reserves for the embryo.

3. Embryo is a new plant candidate that joins two separate cotyledons, the embryo has a part attached to the cotyledons called the embryo shaft.

4. Epicotyl is the part of the embryo that lies above the embryo axis.

5. Plumula is the leaf and stem candidate in the embryo which is located at the end of the epicotyl.

6. Hypocotyl is the part of the embryo that lies below the embryo axis.

7. Radicle is the root candidate in the embryo which is located at the end of the hypocotyl.

B. Part of monocot seeds (hypogeal type)

1. Endosperm is the largest part of the monocot seed, which functions as a food reserve for the embryo.

2. Scutellum is a modification of the cotyledons which form a special structure and function to absorb food from the endosperm to be given to the embryo.

3. Embryo is a new plant candidate.

4. Plumula is the upper end of the embryo which will develop into leaves and stems.

5. Koleoptyl is a structure that serves to protect plumules.

6. Radicle is the lower end of the embryo which will develop into roots.

7. Choleorrhiza is a structure that functions to protect radicles.

There is a difference between cotyledons and endosperm for dicots and monocots. These differences include:

1. In monocots, cotyledons are useful in absorption of food, while endosperm functions as food storage tissue.

2. Generally, in mature seeds of dicots, cotyledons are present while endosperm is absent.

3. Endosperm originates by the union of the male nucleus and the polar nucleus of the embryo sac, whereas the cotyledons are not.

4. In dicotyledons, the endosperm is fully digested before the seeds germinate, while the cotyledons remain until the seeds are capable of photosynthesis.

5. In dicots, cotyledons completely absorb the food stored in the endosperm.

11. Apa pengertian dan tujuan dilakukannya seed treatment


Perlakuan benih atau seed treatment merupakan istilah umum metode aplikasi pestisida yakni ketika pestisida dicampur benih yang akan ditanam. Tujuan perlakuan benih antara lain :
1. Perlakuan benih befungsi sebagai penyuci hama benih (seed strelilant). Melindungi benih agar tidak terkontaminasi oleh bibit hama dan/atau penyakit yang mungkin dibawanya.
2. Perlakuan benih sebagai pelindung benih (Seed protectant). Melindungi benih yang baru ditanam agar tidak dirusak oleh organisme pengganggu tanaman (OPT) seperti semut, anjing tanah, dan hama perusak benih lainnya).
3. Perlakuan benih sebagai pelindung kecambah dan tanaman muda (Seed protectan). Melindungi tanaman muda agar tidak diserang oleh hama/penyakit yang menyerang kecambah atau tanaman muda.

25. penggolongan Seed treatment


Penggolongan Pestisida Berdasarkan OPT (Organisme Pengganggu Tanaman)1.         Insektisida adalah bahan yang mengandung senyawa kimia yang bisa mematikan semua jenis serangga.2.         Fungisida adalah bahan yang mengandung senyawa kimia beracun dan bisa digunakan untuk memberantas dan mencegah fungsi/cendawan.3.         Bakterisida merupakan senyawa mengandung bahan aktif beracun yang bisa membunuh bakteri.4.         Nermatisida digunakan untuk mengendalikan nematode/cacing5.         Akarisida atau mitisida adalah bahan yang mengandung senyawa kimia yang digunakan untuk membunuh tungau, caplak dan laba-laba.6.         Rodenstisida adalah bahan yang mengandung senyawa kimia beracun yang digunakan untuk mematikan berbagai jenis binatang pengerat, misalnya tikus.7.         Moluskisida adalah pestisida untuk membunuh moluska, yaitu : siput, bekicot serta tripisan yang banyak dijumpai di tambak.8.         Herbisida adalah senyawa kimia beracun yang dimanfaatkan untuk membunuh tumbuhan pengganggu seperti gulma.9.         Algasida digunakan untuk mengendalikan ganggang (algae).10.     Pilkisida digunakan untuk mengendalikan ikan buas.11.     Avisida digunakan untuk meracuni burung perusak hasil pertanian.12.     Antraktan digunakan untuk menarik atau mengumpulkan serangga.13.     ZPT digunakan untuk mengatur pertumbuhan tanaman yang efeknya bisa memacu pertumbuhan atau menekan pertumbuhan.14.     Plant Activator digunakan untuk merangsang timbulnya kekebalan tumbuhan sehingga tahan terhadap penyakit tertentu.
