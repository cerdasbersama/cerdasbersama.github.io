3. thank you verymuch for you......., without your help,i could have lost my life​


Jawaban:

Help

Penjelasan:

If you have any more questions or concerns, feel free to ask.

Jawaban:

Possessive Adjectives:

Thank you very much for your help, without your help, I could have lost my life​

Penjelasan:

Jawaban dicetak tebal dan merupakan materi pembelajaran Possessive Adjectives (kata sifat kepemilikan) dalam Bahasa Inggris.

Setelah ungkapan "Thank you for" maka harus diikuti oleh Possessive Pronouns yang mana dalam hal ini adalah "your" kemudian diikuti dengan kata bendanya yaitu "help" yang berarti bantuan atau pertolongan, sehingga menjadi:

Thank you very much for your help, without your help, I could have lost my life​ (Terima kasih banyak atas bantuanmu / pertolonganmu, tanpa bantuanmu / pertolonganmu, aku bisa kehilangan hidupku)

Semoga membantu ya.

Modals - 'must' and 'mustn't'

Do you want to practise using 'must' and 'mustn't' in English? Play our grammar games and have fun while you learn.

Help

Grammar Rule

Examples

I must do my homework.

You mustn’t be late for class.

We must wear our school uniform.

Remember!

We can use ‘must’ to talk about rules. For the negative, we can say ‘must not’ or ‘mustn’t’.

Parents must take care of their children.

You mustn’t shout in the library.

She must not forget her books.

Be careful!

The main verb is without ‘to’.

He must be careful when cutting food.

I mustn’t make a mess.

We say... We don’t say...

You must look both ways when crossing the road. (NOT You must to look both ways when crossing the road.)

We mustn’t run in school. (NOT We mustn’t to run in school.)

Children must respect their parents. (NOT Children must respecting their parents.)

18. You have never dan "you never" bedanya apa?​


Jawaban:

"There is a difference between "you have never" and "you never."

"You have never" is a phrase that implies a lack of experience or occurrence of something up until the present moment. It suggests that a particular action or event has not happened at any time in the past leading up to the present. For example, "You have never been to Paris," means that the person being addressed has not visited Paris at any point in their life until now.

On the other hand, "You never" is a statement that refers to a habitual or recurring action or behavior. It indicates that someone does not or did not perform a specific action regularly or at any time in the past. For instance, "You never clean your room," implies that the person being spoken to has a habit of not cleaning their room consistently.

In summary, "you have never" refers to the absence of an experience or occurrence up until the present, while "you never" describes a recurring action or behavior.
